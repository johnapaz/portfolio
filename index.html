Stanford 

Company overview:

Stanford University is one of the leading research and technology institutions in the world. Areas of excellence range from the humanities to social sciences to engineering and the sciences. Located in the Palo Alto, the Bay Area in California, it is culturally diverse, progressive, rich in talented and intellectuals.

Problem:

The client was responsible for collecting and allocating donation funds for the entire university. With financial resources from the federal government declining, tuition costs high, half of Stanford's undergraduate student body depend on need-based aid.

Stanford depends on gifts in order to achieve their goals. The Office of Development felt their site to collect donations could use improvements.

Their primary concerns were the main landing page and Giving Wizard. These websites felt outdated and didn't properly engage potential donors. The goal was to increase engagement with potential donors to encourage more giving, increase the ease of use to avoid losing users midway through the wizard and redesign both sites to have a more modern look and feel.

My Contribution:

- I acted primarily as the point of contact with the client, after the initial project kickoff.

- I conducted several rounds of stakeholder interviews 11 members.

- We conducted heuristic evaluations of their existing website and donation wizard and then combined our findings.

- We conducted user interviews with Alumni.

- Conducted competitor analysis.

- Diagnosed problems site navigation and information architecture.

- Created sketches of different redesigns of their websites.

- Conducted design thinking exercises with my colleagues to explore solutions, including user journeys, empathy maps, and creating user personas.

- Created a detailed project summary that was delivered to the client, that included a summary of all the findings from my colleagues and myself, mockups we did.
